## Section A — Multiple Choice

**1.** Correct: **A) 35° and 55°** (35 + 55 = 90)
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Complementary Angles
Misconceptions:
- B (55°,125°) — sums to 180°; confuses complementary (sum 90°) with supplementary (sum 180°).
- C (55°,55°) — assumes complementary angles must be equal, over-applying the "equal angles" rule from vertically opposite angles.
- D (55°,45°) — sums to 100°; found the pair by subtracting from 100 instead of 90 — arithmetic slip.

**2.** Correct: **A) 68° and 112°** (68 + 112 = 180)
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Supplementary Angles
Misconceptions:
- B (68°,22°) — sums to 90°; confuses supplementary with complementary.
- C (68°,122°) — sums to 190°; arithmetic slip finding the supplement of 68°.
- D (100°,100°) — assumes a supplementary pair must be two equal angles, wrongly transferring the vertically-opposite "equal" property.

**3.** Correct: **A) ∠AOB and ∠BOC**
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Adjacent Angles
Misconceptions:
- B (∠AOB, ∠AOC) — ∠AOB lies entirely inside ∠AOC; student checks shared vertex/arm but ignores the non-overlapping condition.
- C (∠AOC, ∠BOC) — same overlap error as B, checked from the other arm.
- D (∠BOC, ∠PO′Q) — the two angles share no vertex at all; student matches angles by appearance without checking a common vertex.

**4.** Correct: **A) adjacent angles summing to 180°, a linear pair**
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Linear Pair
Misconceptions:
- B — confuses a ray-on-a-line configuration with vertically opposite angles, which need two intersecting *lines*, not one ray standing on a line.
- C — confuses the linear pair's 180° sum with the complementary 90° sum.
- D — fails to see that the sum is fixed at 180° by the straight line, regardless of where C sits (only the individual measures vary).

**5.** Correct: **A) 65°**
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Vertically Opposite Angles
Misconceptions:
- B (115°) — computed the linear-pair angle (180 − 65) instead of the vertically opposite one.
- C (25°) — treated the pair as complementary (90 − 65) instead of equal.
- D (130°) — doubled the given angle, assuming vertically opposite angles add rather than being equal.

**6.** Correct: **A) Angle 6**
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Transversal of a Line, Corresponding Angles
Misconceptions:
- B (Angle 4) — vertically opposite to angle 2 at the *same* intersection G; confuses vertically opposite with corresponding.
- C (Angle 8) — occupies the diagonal (alternate) position at H; confuses alternate with corresponding.
- D (Angle 3) — same intersection G as angle 2 (adjacent/supplementary); doesn't look at the second intersection H at all.

## Section B — Short Answer

**1.** x + (x + 20) = 90 → 2x = 70 → x = 35. Angles: **35° and 55°**.
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Complementary Angles, worked example.

**2.** (2x+10) + (3x+20) = 180 → 5x = 150 → x = 30. ∠AOB = 70°, ∠BOC = 110°.
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Linear Pair, sum of angles on a straight line.

**3.** Interior angles: ∠3, ∠4 (at P), ∠5, ∠6 (at Q). One valid alternate interior pair: **∠4 & ∠6** (or ∠3 & ∠5) — both between the two lines, on opposite sides of the transversal. Difference from corresponding angles: alternate angles lie on *opposite* sides of the transversal; corresponding angles lie on the *same* side, in matching position at each intersection (e.g. ∠1 & ∠5).
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Angles Made by a Transversal (alternate vs. corresponding).

**4.** 115° + 68° = 183° ≠ 180°. Co-interior angles are not supplementary, so **l and m are NOT parallel**.
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Condition for Parallel Lines using Co-interior Angles.

**5.** ∠PEB and ∠EFD occupy matching positions on the same side of the transversal → corresponding angles. Since AB ∥ CD, corresponding angles are equal: **∠EFD = 72°**.
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Corresponding Angles Axiom for Parallel Lines.

## Section C — Numerical

**1.** (3x+15) + (2x+25) = 180 → 5x = 140 → x = 28.
∠POR = 99°, ∠ROQ = 81°. Vertically opposite: ∠QOS = 99°, ∠SOP = 81°. (Sum around point = 360° ✓)
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Linear Pair and Vertically Opposite Angles.

**2.** ∠3 = 65°.
(a) ∠3 and ∠5 lie between the two lines, on opposite sides of the transversal → alternate interior angles → **∠5 = 65°**.
(b) ∠3 and ∠6 lie between the two lines, on the same side of the transversal → co-interior angles, supplementary → **∠6 = 180 − 65 = 115°**.
Source: NCERT Cl. VII Maths, Ch. 5 — Alternate Interior and Co-interior Angles for Parallel Lines.

## Reteach Note

- If students miss **Q1–Q2 (Section A)**, they likely do not distinguish complementary (90°) from supplementary (180°) sums — reteach the two definitions side by side with a numeric drill.
- If students miss **Q3 (Section A)** or **Q3 (Section B)**, they likely do not check the "opposite sides of the transversal" condition — they can name angle pairs but not verify position, confusing alternate with corresponding or interior with exterior.
- If students miss **Q6 (Section A)**, **Q5 (Section B)**, or **Q2 (Section C)**, they likely have not internalised that parallel-line angle rules require matching a *position* across two separate intersection points, not just reading angle numbers — reteach with a single large labelled diagram left on the board for a full lesson.
