## Section A — Multiple Choice                                                    [6]

**1.** Which pair of angles given below is a pair of complementary angles?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) 35° and 55°  &nbsp;&nbsp;B) 55° and 125°  &nbsp;&nbsp;C) 55° and 55°  &nbsp;&nbsp;D) 55° and 45°

**2.** Which pair of angles given below is a pair of supplementary angles?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) 68° and 112°  &nbsp;&nbsp;B) 68° and 22°  &nbsp;&nbsp;C) 68° and 122°  &nbsp;&nbsp;D) 100° and 100°

**3.** At point O, three rays OA, OB and OC are drawn such that ray OB lies between rays OA and OC, dividing ∠AOC into ∠AOB and ∠BOC. At a different point O′ in the same figure, two more rays O′P and O′Q form ∠PO′Q. Which pair of angles satisfies **all** the conditions for adjacent angles (common vertex, common arm, non-overlapping interiors, opposite sides of the common arm)?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) ∠AOB and ∠BOC  &nbsp;&nbsp;B) ∠AOB and ∠AOC  &nbsp;&nbsp;C) ∠AOC and ∠BOC  &nbsp;&nbsp;D) ∠BOC and ∠PO′Q

**4.** Ray OC stands on a straight line AOB (A, O, B collinear), with OC starting from O between A and B, forming ∠AOC and ∠BOC. Which statement correctly describes these two angles?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) They are adjacent angles whose measures add up to 180°, forming a linear pair
&nbsp;&nbsp;B) They are vertically opposite angles and are therefore always equal in measure
&nbsp;&nbsp;C) They are complementary angles whose measures add up to 90°
&nbsp;&nbsp;D) Their sum cannot be determined unless the exact position of ray OC is known

**5.** Lines PQ and RS intersect at point O, forming four angles at O. If ∠POR = 65°, what is the measure of ∠QOS, the angle vertically opposite to ∠POR?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) 65°  &nbsp;&nbsp;B) 115°  &nbsp;&nbsp;C) 25°  &nbsp;&nbsp;D) 130°

**6.** A transversal EF cuts two parallel lines AB and CD at points G and H. At G, the four angles are numbered 1, 2, 3, 4 clockwise starting top-left. At H, the four angles are numbered 5, 6, 7, 8 in the same clockwise order starting top-left. Which angle is the corresponding angle to angle 2?                                   [1]
&nbsp;&nbsp;A) Angle 6  &nbsp;&nbsp;B) Angle 4  &nbsp;&nbsp;C) Angle 8  &nbsp;&nbsp;D) Angle 3

## Section B — Short Answer                                                      [10]

**1.** Two angles are complementary. One angle measures x° and the other measures (x + 20)°. Find the measure of each angle. Show your working.                                   [2]

**2.** Points A, O and C lie on a straight line. Ray OB lies between OA and OC, such that ∠AOB = (2x + 10)° and ∠BOC = (3x + 20)°. Find the value of x and the measure of each angle.                                   [2]

**3.** A transversal t cuts two lines l and m at points P and Q (P above Q). At P, the angles are labelled clockwise from top-left as ∠1, ∠2, ∠3, ∠4. At Q, the angles are labelled clockwise from top-left as ∠5, ∠6, ∠7, ∠8. Name **one** pair of alternate interior angles from this figure, and explain in one sentence how alternate angles differ in position from corresponding angles.                                   [2]

**4.** Two lines l and m are cut by a transversal t. The interior angle at l measures 115°, and the co-interior (allied) angle at m measures 68°. Using the co-interior angles condition, determine whether l and m are parallel. Justify your answer.                                   [2]

**5.** Lines AB and CD are parallel. A transversal PQ cuts AB at E and CD at F, with P above E and Q below F on the transversal. Ray EA points left, ray EB points right; ray FC points left (same direction as EA), ray FD points right (same direction as EB). Given ∠PEB = 72°, find ∠EFD, stating the property used.                                   [2]

**2.** Lines l and m are parallel, cut by transversal t at P (on l) and Q (on m), P above Q. Angles at P are labelled clockwise from top-left as ∠1, ∠2, ∠3, ∠4; angles at Q as ∠5, ∠6, ∠7, ∠8, same convention. Given ∠3 = 65°, find (a) ∠5 using the alternate interior angles property, and (b) ∠6 using the co-interior angles property. State the property used in each part.                                   [2]
